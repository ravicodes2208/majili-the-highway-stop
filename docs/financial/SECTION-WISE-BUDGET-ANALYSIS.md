# Section-Wise Budget & Break-Even Analysis
## Highway Coffee Rest Stop — NH-365BB, Jeellacheruvu

---

## 1. Sections Defined

A highway rest stop serves passengers who stop for 10-15 minutes. Every section
must be **quick-serve** (under 3 minutes). Based on highway traveler behavior:

| Section | What It Covers | Why Travelers Order It |
|---------|---------------|----------------------|
| **A. Hot Beverages** | Filter coffee, tea varieties, badam milk, hot chocolate | Primary reason to stop. Refreshment + energy. Ordered by ~80% of customers |
| **B. Cold Beverages** | Cold coffee, milkshakes, fresh lime soda, buttermilk, lassi | Summer essential. Highest margin section. Ordered by ~25% of customers |
| **C. Snacks** | Samosa, vada, bajji, punugulu, sweet corn, fries | Pairs with beverages. Quick hunger fix. Ordered by ~50% of customers |
| **D. Packaged Items** | Water bottles, soft drinks, chips, biscuits | Grab-and-go. Lowest margin but zero prep effort. Ordered by ~30% of customers |
| **E. Toilets & Rest Area** | Clean toilets (3T + 5U), seating, parking | NOT a revenue section — this is your TRAFFIC MAGNET. Drives all other sections |

> **Section E (Toilets)** generates zero revenue but is the #1 reason families stop.
> Its cost is distributed across Sections A-D as overhead.

## 3. CapEx Allocation to Sections

### Methodology

Each rupee of CapEx is allocated to sections using a blended approach:

- **Direct Equipment** (40% weight): Equipment that exclusively serves one section
  goes 100% to that section
- **Shared Infrastructure** (60% weight): Building, seating, utilities, signage, etc.
  allocated by expected **revenue contribution** of each section

**Expected Revenue Contribution:**
| Section | Revenue Share | Basis |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Hot Beverages | 45% | Anchor product, highest volume |
| Cold Beverages | 18% | Premium pricing, seasonal demand |
| Snacks | 27% | Strong pairing with beverages |
| Packaged | 10% | Low margin, high convenience |

### 9.2 Section-Level Break-Even (Customers/Day)

**Formula**: Break-even customers = Break-even servings / (% customers who order × avg items)

| Section | Break-Even Servings | Order Rate | Avg Items/Order | **Break-Even Customers/Day** |
|---------|--------------------|-----------|-----------------|-----------------------------|
